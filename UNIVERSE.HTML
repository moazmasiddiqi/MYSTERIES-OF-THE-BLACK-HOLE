Black Holes: The Cosmic Monsters

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They form when massive stars collapse under their own gravity. Black holes come in different sizes, from stellar-mass to supermassive giants at the centers of galaxies, including our Milky Way. Studying them helps us understand the limits of physics, time, and space itself.

The Mysterious Sound of a Black Hole

In 2022, NASA released an eerie audio recording of sound waves captured from a black hole in the Perseus galaxy cluster. These waves were converted into audible frequencies, giving us a rare chance to "hear" the cosmos. The sound is deep, haunting, and unlike anything on Earth — a reminder of the unimaginable forces at play in the universe.
